- Main limitation acknowledged by authors: The analysis is latency/reaction-time centric and does not solve fault tolerance or broader multi-objective placement needs.
- Data regime (size, quality, realism): Real/public WAN topology sets (100+ networks) with measured propagation-latency analysis.
- Evaluation setting (controlled, synthetic, real-world): Controlled offline evaluation on real topology datasets, not live deployment.

### Technological SotA
- Best algorithms/architectures: Facility-location style latency optimization (k-median and k-center framing) with exhaustive controller-combination search.

### Methodological SotA
- Training/validation method: Deterministic metric computation of average and worst-case latency over candidate controller sets.

### Functional SotA
- What the system can do today: Quantify how much placement quality depends on topology and controller count.

## 4) SotA vs Gap Test

- SotA statement: Foundational CPP methods can strongly reduce latency and provide clear lower-bound placement intuition.
- Gap statement: The paper does not provide a reproducible, multi-objective benchmark that includes compute burden and reliability trade-offs.
- Why this gap matters for SDN controller placement research: Modern controller placement decisions require latency-quality gains to be judged alongside runtime and resilience, not as a single-metric optimization.
